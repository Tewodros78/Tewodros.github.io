Research

Mathematical and statistical modeling of infectious diseases, with a focus on HIV.

HIV Drug Resistance Dynamics

My research focuses on developing and analyzing mathematical models of HIV viral dynamics in the context of prevention strategies, particularly pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP). I am broadly interested in how drug action influences viral establishment, early infection dynamics, and the likelihood of preventing HIV acquisition. By integrating PK/PD processes with HIV transmission and early viral dynamics, my work aims to evaluate the effectiveness of PrEP regimens, quantify the probability of infection under different dosing strategies, and explore how pharmacological factors can improve prevention outcomes.

Biostatistical Applications in Maternal and Child Health

My previous work in biostatistics includes modeling teenage fertility trends and identifying predictors of hospital stay length among preterm infants in Ethiopia. These projects involved regression modeling, survival and competing risk analysis, and careful interpretation of results for clinical and policy audiences.
