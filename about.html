About

Economic Modeling · Public Health Economics · Quantitative Research

Ph.D. Student in Economics · Howard University

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Economics at Howard University with a strong interest in applying quantitative and computational methods to address public health and socioeconomic challenges in under-resourced communities. My research focuses on developing economic and statistical models of health and behavioral dynamics—with a special emphasis on HIV treatment outcomes, drug resistance, and the economic factors that shape long-term health decisions.

Before beginning my doctoral studies, I completed a Master of Science in Biostatistics and a Bachelor of Science in Statistics at Addis Ababa University. These experiences strengthened my foundation in quantitative research, statistical analysis, and the use of computational tools for real-world health and economic datasets.

I enjoy working at the intersection of economics, public health, and quantitative modeling—analyzing data, developing models, and collaborating with researchers and practitioners to inform policy and improve population-level outcomes.
