About

Economics Modeling · Microeconomics · Public Health

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Mathematics at Howard University, where I focus on developing mathematical and statistical models to better understand infectious disease dynamics. My research centers on HIV drug resistance and treatment outcomes, using differential equations, numerical simulation, and quantitative methods to explore how therapies influence long-term viral behavior.

Before beginning my Ph.D. studies, I completed a Master of Science in Biostatistics and a Bachelor of Science in Statistics at Addis Ababa University. During this time, I gained extensive experience in applied statistics, data analysis, and research design, working with real-world health and demographic datasets.

My broader interest lies at the intersection of mathematics, public health, and data science. I enjoy building and analyzing models, working with data, and collaborating with researchers and clinicians to generate insights that can inform policy and improve patient care, especially in underserved communities.
