Tewodros G. Gutema

Ph.D. Student in Economics · Howard University

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Economics at Howard University with a strong interest in applying quantitative and computational methods to address public health and socioeconomic challenges in under-resourced communities. My research focuses on developing economic and statistical models of health and behavioral dynamics—with a particular emphasis on HIV treatment outcomes, drug resistance, and the economic factors shaping long-term decision-making.

About Me

My academic interests lie at the intersection of economics, public health, and quantitative modeling. I am driven by the potential of economic theory and statistical modeling to explain health behaviors, guide policy decisions, and improve health equity—particularly in underserved populations.
